From Cinematographer to Event Manager

A Film Set is a Live Event

For 14 years, my job has been to turn a creative vision into a tangible reality. Every film shoot is a high-stakes event with complex logistics, tight deadlines, and diverse teams, equipping me with a robust skillset perfectly suited for event management.

Vision & Planning

THE FILM SET

Translating a script into a visual blueprint. Creating storyboards, shot lists, and lighting plans to guide the entire production.

THE EVENT

Defining an event's purpose and goals. Developing a cohesive concept, curating content, and creating a detailed agenda for execution.

Team Management

THE FILM SET

Leading a crew of technicians and artists while collaborating closely with directors and producers to meet client expectations.

THE EVENT

Coordinating with vendors, speakers, and internal teams, ensuring everyone is aligned and working towards a common goal.

Communication

THE FILM SET

Acting as the key liaison between the director's vision and the technical crew. Clearly articulating complex creative and technical needs under pressure.

THE EVENT

Serving as the central point of contact for all stakeholders. Providing clear updates and managing expectations for clients, suppliers, and attendees.

Logistics

THE FILM SET

Managing high-value equipment, scouting and securing locations, and coordinating schedules for cast and crew across multiple departments.

THE EVENT

Handling venue booking, AV setup, and supplier management. Planning timelines and resources to ensure a seamless flow from start to finish.

Execution & Problem-Solving

THE FILM SET

Thinking on my feet to solve technical and creative problems on the fly. Adapting to changing conditions to ensure the project stays on schedule and on budget.

THE EVENT

Proactively managing on-site challenges and making decisive calls. Ensuring a flawless experience for attendees, no matter what happens behind the scenes.
